4‑Week Outdoor Container Study: BSFL Frass vs Compost vs Unamended Soil

Nellie’s Garden Partnership Study with Symton Black Soldier Fly • 24 × 5‑gallon pots • Randomized Complete Block Design (RCBD) • Fast leafy greens

Study 1 Basic Pilot Study Comparing Garden Soil, Compost Blend and Frass Blend.

Study Date: February 2026 to April 2026. Location: Houston, Texas.

Study 1 Protocol

1. Objective

Evaluate whether BSFL frass produces statistically and practically meaningful improvements in early vegetative growth and fresh biomass yield of fast‑growing leafy greens compared with compost and unamended soil over 28 days in an outdoor 5‑gallon container trial.

2. Research question

In outdoor 5‑gallon container culture, does frass‑amended soil produce higher growth and yield outcomes than compost‑amended soil or unamended soil over 28 days?

3. Hypotheses

  • H0: Mean growth and yield are equal across treatments.
  • H1: At least one treatment differs; specifically, frass improves growth and/or yield relative to compost and/or control.

4. Experimental design

Design type: Randomized Complete Block Design (RCBD), outdoor

Blocks: 8 blocks (B01–B08)

Experimental units: 24 pots (5‑gal), 1 pot = 1 unit

Treatments: 3 (T0/T1/T2), n = 8 per treatment

5. Treatments

  • T0 Control: unamended soil (no compost, no frass)
  • T1 Compost: compost amendment at defined mass dose
  • T2 Frass: BSFL frass amendment at same mass dose

6. Amendment dose (mass-based)

Target dose: 1.0% w/w on a dry-soil-equivalent basis. Compost and frass are mass‑matched.

Amendment mass per pot (g) = 0.010 × (estimated dry soil mass per pot, g)

Recommended dry‑equivalent estimation: weigh a ~200 g soil subsample wet → air‑dry to constant weight → compute dry fraction, then apply to each pot’s wet soil mass to estimate dry soil mass.

7. Crop

Choose one fast leafy green for uniformity (recommended: arugula; acceptable: leaf lettuce or baby bok choy).

8. Planting and thinning

  1. Direct seed each pot at the same initial density.
  2. At Day 7, thin to a fixed density (example: 20 plants per pot), evenly spaced.

9. Watering and maintenance

  • Standardize watering across all pots (same schedule and/or equal volumes per event).
  • Log all watering events and deviations (storms, pests, missed watering).
  • No additional fertilizers during the trial.

10. Outcomes

Primary endpoint (Day 28): fresh aboveground biomass (g/pot) harvested using a standardized method.

Weekly secondary endpoints (Days 7, 14, 21, 28):

  • Plant height (cm)
  • Leaf count (or canopy diameter)
  • Color score (1–5)
  • Vigor score (1–5)
  • Pest/disease presence (Y/N)
  • Standardized photos (same angle/distance each time)

Soil functional indicator (Day 0 and Day 28): infiltration time (250 mL water; seconds to infiltrate).

11. Statistical analysis plan

  • Data will be analyzed using a randomized complete block design (RCBD). A linear model (ANOVA) will be fitted with treatment (control, compost, frass) specified as a fixed effect and block included as a blocking factor to account for spatial or environmental variability.
  • When the overall treatment effect is significant, post‑hoc pairwise comparisons among treatments will be conducted using Tukey’s Honestly Significant Difference (HSD) test to control for multiple comparisons.
  • Results will be reported as mean ± standard deviation (SD) for each treatment. Treatment effects will also be expressed as percentage differences relative to the control and compost treatments, along with p‑values and/or confidence intervals to quantify statistical significance and uncertainty.

Addendum: Study 2 Follow‑Up (Frass Tea)

This is intentionally a separate study so Study 1 stays clean and unconfounded.

Purpose

Evaluate whether a standardized aqueous frass extract (“frass tea”) applied as a drench produces measurable short‑term benefits, while controlling for watering volume and timing.

Tea preparation (repeatable aqueous extract)

  • Ratio: 1:10 w/v (example: 100 g frass : 1,000 mL water)
  • Steep: 12–24 hours at ambient temperature
  • Filter: mesh/cloth
  • Use: same day (or within 24 hours)

Application (example)

  • Apply a fixed volume (e.g., 250 mL/pot) on a fixed schedule (e.g., weekly).
  • At that same event, other groups receive the same volume of plain water (volume‑matched).
  • Optional rigor: record tea EC and pH each batch.
